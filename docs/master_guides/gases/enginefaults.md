# Minor and Major Engine Systems Faults

The following lists some of the possible combinations of exhaust gas values and the most likely causes.

| CO | CO2 | HC | O2 | A/F Ratio: possible problems, conditions or causes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Low to Moderate | Low | Low to Moderate | Low | **Major Engine Faults:** Compression Low; Camshaft lobe lift insufficient |
| Low to Moderate | Low | Low to Moderate | Low | **Minor Engine Faults:** Ignition Timing over-advanced; Spark Plug/Wire grounded or open; ECM trying to compensate for a vacuum leak |
| Low | High | Low | High | Injector misfire, catalytic converter operating correctly |
| High | Low | High | Low | Thermostat or coolant temperature sensor faulty — "cold running engine" |
| Low | High | Low | Low | Thermostat or coolant temperature sensor faulty — "hot running engine" |
| Low | Low | Low | High | Exhaust leak after the catalytic converter |
| High | High | High | High | Combination of rich mixture and vacuum leak; injector misfire; catalytic converter not working |
| Low | High | Low | Low | **Good combustion efficiency and catalytic converter working properly!** |

Notes preserved from the original chart:

- The first two rows share an identical gas signature (Low–Moderate CO, Low CO2, Low–Moderate HC, Low O2) but split by severity: low compression / insufficient cam lift versus over-advanced timing or ECM compensation for a vacuum leak.
- The "good combustion" row and the "hot running engine" row share Low CO + High CO2 + Low HC + Low O2 — they are distinguished by the temperature behaviour and trim pattern of the engine, not the gases alone.
- The "exhaust leak after cat" row shows Low CO, Low CO2, Low HC, High O2 — the leak dilutes the post-cat sample with ambient air, dropping every combustion product while raising O2.
- The all-High row (CO + CO2 + HC + O2 all high) is the canonical "everything is broken at once" pattern: rich mixture *and* vacuum leak *and* injector misfire *and* dead cat.
